Malayan tiger

The Malayan tiger has been considered an critically endangered animal since 2015. As of year 2022, it is determined that there is less than 150 individuals and is continuing to reduce in numbers due to several reasons. The 2 main reasons are given as loss of habitat and illegal poaching. Due to development projects, the tiger's habitat, natural forest in Peninsula Malaysia has decrease in size rapidly. Besides that, tiger body parts are also considered a rare traditional medicine in certain cultures, therefore it is being threatened by poachers ever since. Furthermore, tiger skins can be manufactured into expensive cosmetics which are very popular in the rich. In short, we need to provide as much aid to the Malayan tigers as we can to prevent its extinction.


Bornean orangutans

The Bornean Orangutans has been in a critically endangered status due to its rapid population decrease by 60% since the 1950s. Orangutans plays an important roles in maintaining the biodiversity of the jungle as they help in seed dispersing which keeps the jungle healthy. The current main threat towards the orangutans is illegal logging. Due to agriculture needs, large area of lands are needed. Therefore, some unresponsible companies burn down forests to save costs for logging and caused many orangutans to burn alive in forest without being able to escape. Deforestation also causes orangutans to lose their habtat and decrease in numbers quickly. Sometimes, young orangutans are also hunted down to be sold for pet trading in certain places. In conclusion, we should help raise awareness on the importance of orangutans before its extinction.


Sea Turtle

The sea turtles are also a critically endangered species since its massive population decline. One of the turtle species in Malaysia, the leatherback turtle has decreased by more than 99% since 1960s. It has also been shown that no leatherback turtle has ever shown nesting in Malaysia since 2017. There are currently three main threats being faced by the sea turtles in our country. The first one being turtle eggs being collected for consumption and trading. This causes the turtle population unable to increase in numbers. The second threat is the trash in oceans. There have been many cases where turtles are caught in nets and unable to surface to breathe, which results in them drowning. There are certain cases where turtles wrongly feast on plastics and gets poisoned. The third threat is climate change which affects the sea ecosystem. In this scenario, we can help by working as a volunteer or donating to research and conservation centres which helps in monitoring the breeding of turtles.


Bornean elephant

The Bornean elephants has been classified as an endangered species with only less than 1500 individuals alive today. It has been determined that the population of Bornean elephants has decreased by a minimum of 50% over 3 generations. There are a few threats which are being faced by Bornean elephants right now. The main threat being conflict with humans. As the country continues to develop, lands are not only needed for agriculture, but also infrustructure. This causes the elephants to lose their habitats. Sometimes, elephants may need to travel through plantations to reach their destinations and this causes them to trample over the plantation. This results in conflicts between the elephants and humans, and in serious cases, elephnats are shot down by humans. Other than that, due to deforestation, food source has also been a huge problem for the Bornean elephants as their main food source are plants. To conclude, we can help by donating to conservation centers which helps in monitoring the elephant's habitat in order to reduce elephant-human conflict.
